Born A Crime






Born a crime explains how apartheid in South Africa separated the country into two tribes the Zuluand the Xhosa.There was warfare among these two tribes . The fierce Zulu tribe were wiped out after they resisted European invaders. On the other hand, the Xhosa people made an effort to absorb lessonsfrom the Europeans and used strategy rather than force to repel their invasion. When apartheid ended, the two tribes' growing resentment turned into a battle. Trevor Noah's mother, was of Xhosa desc. Trevor Noah narrates a story about the history, customs, and culture of his own nation during theyears just prior to and following the end of apartheid.Trevor's mother was a devoted Christian who took her family to several churches every Sunday, despite the fact that native South Africans had been forced to convert to Christianity by European settlers.

Every church had a certain racial demographic—primarily Black, mostly White, or mixed—and offered something unique. Trevor loved going to church, but he didn't enjoy the commute to these churches.When the family's used vehicle broke down in the mornings, they would have to take different minibusses. Trevor's mother had unshakable trust in God and was never easily intimidated.When Trevor was nine years old, his mother received threatening remarks from a bus driverwho accused her of being a prostitute and condemning her for traveling with a stranger .

Trevor was born into a family of Black parents and White fathers amid the legal prohibition of interracial marriage during the apartheid era. .
